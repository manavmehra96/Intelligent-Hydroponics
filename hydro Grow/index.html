What is Hydroponics

Hydroponics is a subset of hydroculture, the method of growing plants without soil, using mineral nutrient solutions in a water solvent.[1] Terrestrial plants may be grown with only their roots exposed to the mineral solution, or the roots may be supported by an inert medium, such as perlite or gravel. The nutrients in hydroponics can come from an array of different sources; these can include but are not limited to byproduct from fish waste, duck manure, or normal nutrients.

Why Hydroponics?

Through hydroponic gardening; plants can be grown anywhere as long as their growth requirements are met.
It uses only 1/20th of water compared to traditional gardening.
Plants grown through this technique are healthy and have better nutritional value. It has been proved that vitamin content is 50% more in hydroponically grown plants as compared to conventional ones.
There are no worries about the changing seasons, as crops can be grown all year round.

Problem Statement and Proposed Methodology

To develop a prototype for Intelligent IoT based Hydro-Aeroponic Agriculture System for harsh climatic conditions.

Identify aeroponics and hydroponic friendly plants and procure specific nutrients required. Procure the data sets and accordingly apply the appropriate machine learning algorithm in training the data set towards prediction for regulating the nutrients and oxygen required by the plant based on Ph, Temperature, lighting, humidity, etc. Development of a user friendly mobile application across android and iOS with an added functionality of a chatbot which can send out timely notifications and can be a mediator between the system and the user. Integrate the entire system with all the modules to develop a smart solution of aeroponics and hydroponic systems for harsh climatic conditions.

Drawbacks of Existing System

Spread of diseases and pests due to plants grown under close lid systems that escalates to all crops under the same nutrient reservoir. Frequent System Failure due to power outage resulting in dry and unhealthy crops. Hydroponics has not been promoted in India due to the lack of technical knowledge, experience and availability of capital funds. The present system is incapable of growing crops using hydroponics technology in harsh climatic conditions. Inability of growing specific crops with the existing technology.
